The Ultimate Guide to Minimalist Desk Setups

Sarah Jenkins

Editor in Chief • Oct 12, 2026

"Clutter is nothing more than postponed decisions." — In this guide, we break down how to reclaim your mental space by organizing your physical one.

1. Start with the Essentials

The first step to a minimalist setup is removing everything that isn't essential. Look at your desk. If you haven't used an item in the last week, put it in a drawer.

In our October Tech Box, we included a magnetic cable organizer specifically for this purpose. Keeping cables off the surface immediately makes the space feel 50% larger.

Pro Tip: Use a monitor arm to lift your screen off the desk. This reclaims prime real estate for your notebook or coffee.

2. Lighting Matters

Bad lighting causes eye strain, which kills productivity. A simple bias lighting strip behind your monitor (available in the "Gamer" tier subscription) can reduce contrast strain.

Conclusion

Minimalism isn't about having nothing; it's about having no distractions. Start small, remove one item today, and see how your focus improves.
